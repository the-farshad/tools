15 October 2012

Founding of the HDP

Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)

A pluralist project

On 15 October 2012, the Halkların Demokratik Partisi (HDP / Peoples' Democratic Party) was founded in Turkey as a successor to a string of pro-Kurdish parties (HEP, DEP, HADEP, DEHAP, DTP, BDP) that had been repeatedly closed by the Constitutional Court. The HDP brought together Kurdish, leftist, feminist, environmentalist, and minority constituencies under a single platform.

It crossed Turkey's 10% electoral threshold in 2015, winning 80 seats in parliament. Most of its co-leaders — including Selahattin Demirtaş and Figen Yüksekdağ — have been imprisoned since 2016.

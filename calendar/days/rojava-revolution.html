19 July 2012

Rojava Revolution

Beginning of autonomous administration in Kurdish-majority northeast Syria

Şoreşa Rojava

On 19 July 2012, as the Syrian civil war fragmented government control, Kurdish-led forces and local councils established autonomous administration in three predominantly Kurdish cantons of northern Syria — Afrîn, Kobanê, and Cizîrê. The movement adopted the framework of democratic confederalism: local councils, women's co-leadership, and the inclusion of Arabs, Assyrians, Armenians, Turkmens, and Yezidîs.

The autonomous region became known as Rojavayê Kurdistanê ("western Kurdistan") and later, after expansion, the Autonomous Administration of North and East Syria (AANES).

Jin, Jîyan, Azadî. — Woman, Life, Freedom.

What it produced

Among other things: a constitution emphasizing pluralism, a women's defence unit (YPJ) that became internationally known during the defence of Kobanê against ISIS, mother-tongue education in Kurdish, Arabic, and Syriac, and one of the most actively documented experiments in stateless self-government in the modern Middle East.

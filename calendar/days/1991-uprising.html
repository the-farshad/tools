5 March 1991

Beginning of the 1991 Kurdish uprising

Iraqi Kurds rise after the Gulf War

Raperîn

On 5 March 1991, two weeks after the end of the Gulf War, an uprising (Raperîn) broke out in Rania and spread within days across Iraqi Kurdistan. By mid-March, Kurdish forces controlled almost every major Kurdish-majority city, including Kirkuk and Erbil.

Saddam Hussein's regime, having survived the Gulf War, struck back hard within weeks. Nearly two million people fled toward the Iranian and Turkish borders in one of the largest refugee movements of the late 20th century. Their plight prompted the establishment of the no-fly zone in April, which in turn made the autonomous Iraqi Kurdish government possible.

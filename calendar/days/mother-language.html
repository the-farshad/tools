21 February

Mother Language Day

International day for linguistic and cultural diversity

Why this day

The United Nations declared 21 February International Mother Language Day to commemorate the 1952 Bengali language movement, in which students in Dhaka were killed defending the right to their language.

For Kurds the day is observed widely as a moment to celebrate Kurdish in all its varieties (Kurmanji, Sorani, Kelhuri, Hewrami, Zazaki) and to remember the long history of restrictions on speaking, teaching, and publishing in Kurdish.

A language is a way of seeing the world. Lose the language and a whole way of seeing goes with it.

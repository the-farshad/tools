Anfal Memorial

In remembrance of the Anfal campaign of 1986–1989

A campaign of erasure

The Anfal campaign was a series of military operations conducted by the Iraqi government against the Kurdish population of northern Iraq. Between 1986 and 1989, an estimated 50,000 to 182,000 Kurds were killed, more than 4,000 villages were destroyed, and chemical weapons were used against civilians. Halabja was one episode within Anfal.

To remember Anfal is to refuse the silence its planners hoped for.

Recognition

The Anfal campaign has been recognized as genocide by Iraq, the United Kingdom, Sweden, Norway, and South Korea. Survivors and researchers continue to document mass-grave sites uncovered after 2003.

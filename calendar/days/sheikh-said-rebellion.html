13 February 1925

Beginning of the Sheikh Said rebellion

Kurdish uprising in eastern Turkey

Pîran, February 1925

On 13 February 1925, an incident in the village of Pîran (Dicle, Diyarbakır) escalated into the first large Kurdish uprising against the new Republic of Turkey. Led by the Naqshbandi sheikh Şêx Saîd, the rebellion drew together religious, tribal, and nationalist motivations. Within two months it controlled much of southeastern Anatolia.

The rebellion was crushed by April 1925. Şêx Saîd and 46 others were hanged in Diyarbakır on 29 June 1925. The aftermath produced sweeping bans on Kurdish language and identity in Turkey that lasted, in various forms, for most of the 20th century.

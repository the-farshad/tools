5 October 1927

Founding of the Khoybûn League

First transnational Kurdish nationalist organization

Independence in exile

On 5 October 1927, exiled Kurdish intellectuals and former Bedirxan officers met in Bhamdoun, Lebanon to found Xoybûn (Khoybûn) — literally "self-becoming" or "independence." It was the first transnational Kurdish nationalist organization, and it organized the Ararat Rebellion (1930) led by Ihsan Nuri Pasha against the Turkish state.

Khoybûn's intellectual circle — including Celadet Bedirxan and Memduh Selim — would shortly produce the Hawar journal (1932) and the modern Latin alphabet for Northern Kurdish.

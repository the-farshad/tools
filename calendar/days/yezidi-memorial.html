3 August 2014

Yezîdî Genocide Memorial

In remembrance of the victims of the Sinjar attacks

Sinjar

On 3 August 2014, ISIS forces attacked the Yezîdî community in the Sinjar region (Şingal) of northern Iraq. Within days, thousands of men were killed, more than seven thousand women and girls were enslaved and trafficked, and tens of thousands of survivors fled into the Sinjar mountains where they were besieged without food or water for days.

The Yezîdîs, a religious minority indigenous to Kurdistan, had survived 74 firmans — massacres — over their long history. The 2014 attack is widely recognized as the seventy-fourth.

To remember Sinjar is to refuse the silence its perpetrators demanded.

Recognition

The United Nations, the European Parliament, and the parliaments of multiple countries (including the UK, US, France, Germany, Iraq, Belgium, the Netherlands, Australia, Scotland, Armenia, and others) have formally recognized the attack as genocide. The 2018 Nobel Peace Prize was awarded to Yezîdî survivor and activist Nadia Murad for her work on accountability and assistance to survivors.

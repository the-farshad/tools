21 March

Newroz

Kurdish New Year · the first day of spring

The fire on the mountain

Newroz marks the spring equinox and the first day of the year in the Jalali calendar. Fires are lit on hilltops, and people gather in green places to dance, sing, and welcome the new year.

For Kurds the day is also tied to the legend of Kawa the Blacksmith, who, in Ferdowsi's Shahnameh and in Kurdish oral tradition, leads an uprising against the tyrant Zahak and lights a fire on the mountain to signal liberation.

Newroz piroz be! — Happy Newroz.

How it's celebrated

Cities and villages across Kurdistan light bonfires the night before. People dress in colorful traditional clothes, jump over small fires for renewal, and gather for govend (line dance). Picnics in the countryside continue through the following weeks.

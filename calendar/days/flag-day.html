Kurdish Flag Day

Roja Alaya Kurdistanê

Three colors and a sun

The Kurdish flag has three horizontal stripes — red, white, green — with a 21-rayed golden sun in the center. The design dates back to the Republic of Mahabad (1946) and was formally adopted as the official flag of the Kurdistan Region on 17 December 1999.

The 21 rays of the sun are often associated with Newroz (the 21st day of March) and with the long-standing solar symbolism in pre-Islamic Iranian and Mesopotamian cultures.

Bijî alaya me. — Long live our flag.

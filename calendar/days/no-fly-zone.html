7 April 1991

Establishment of the No-Fly Zone

Operation Provide Comfort protects Iraqi Kurdistan

Refuge from the air

In early April 1991, after Saddam Hussein's brutal suppression of the post-Gulf-War Kurdish uprising had pushed nearly two million Kurds toward the Iranian and Turkish borders, the United States, United Kingdom, and France launched Operation Provide Comfort. By 7 April the no-fly zone above the 36th parallel was in force, allowing displaced Kurds to return home and giving room for the autonomous Iraqi Kurdish government that took shape the following year.

22 January 1946

Republic of Mahabad

The first — and only — Kurdish republic of the 20th century

A republic in the mountains

On 22 January 1946, in Mahabad in northwestern Iran, Qazi Muhammad declared the Republic of Kurdistan. Its flag was red, white, and green with a sun in the center; its language of government was Kurdish. The republic lasted only eleven months before Iranian forces re-took the region.

Qazi Muhammad and several colleagues were executed in March 1947 in the same square where the republic had been proclaimed.

Bijî Komara Mahabad. — Long live the Republic of Mahabad.

Why it still matters

Brief as it was, the republic produced the first modern textbooks and newspapers in Kurdish, recognized Kurdish as an official language, and remains a touchstone for Kurdish political memory. Its flag is the basis of the Kurdistan Region's flag today.

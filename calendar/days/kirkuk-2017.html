16 October 2017

Iraqi forces re-take Kirkuk

Aftermath of the Kurdistan independence referendum

Three weeks after the vote

On 16 October 2017, three weeks after the Iraqi Kurdish referendum on independence, Iraqi federal forces and the Hashd al-Shaabi (Popular Mobilization Forces) entered Kirkuk and the surrounding disputed territories with little resistance. Most of the territory the Peshmerga had administered since 2014 was lost in days. The Kurdistan Region's borders rolled back to roughly its pre-2014 lines.

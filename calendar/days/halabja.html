16 March 1988

Halabja

In remembrance of those killed in the chemical attack on Halabja

What happened

On the afternoon of 16 March 1988, Iraqi warplanes dropped a mix of mustard gas and nerve agents on the Kurdish town of Halabja in Iraqi Kurdistan. An estimated 5,000 people were killed within hours; thousands more suffered long-term injuries and birth defects in the years that followed.

It is the largest chemical-weapons attack ever directed against a civilian population. The attack took place during the Anfal campaign at the close of the Iran-Iraq war.

A whole town stopped breathing in an afternoon. We remember by refusing to look away.

Recognition

In 2010 the Iraqi High Criminal Court formally recognized the Halabja attack as an act of genocide against the Kurdish people. 16 March is observed across Kurdistan as a day of remembrance.

23 August 1514

Battle of Chaldiran

Ottoman victory shapes the partition of Kurdistan

Two empires, one dividing line

On 23 August 1514, on the plain of Chaldiran north of Lake Urmia, the Ottoman army of Sultan Selim I defeated the Safavid Shah Ismail I. Most Kurdish emirs sided with the Ottomans, who promised them autonomy in exchange — a settlement negotiated largely by the Kurdish polymath Idris Bitlisi.

Chaldiran inaugurated four centuries of Kurdistan being split between Ottoman and Persian/Iranian spheres — a partition formalized in the Treaty of Zuhab (1639) and that, in much of its modern form, persists today.

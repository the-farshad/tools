First Wednesday of Nisan

Çarşema Sor

Yezidi New Year — the Red Wednesday

Şîrê Şams

Çarşema Sor (Red Wednesday) is the most sacred day in the Yezidi religious calendar — the day on which the angel Tawûsê Melek descended to earth and the world began. It falls on the first Wednesday of Nisan in the Yezidi calendar, which corresponds to mid-April in the Gregorian calendar (currently between 14 and 21 April depending on the year).

Families decorate eggs in bright colours, prepare special foods, light candles in temples, and visit the graves of relatives. In Lalish, the holiest Yezidi shrine, pilgrims gather around the spring of Kaniya Sipî.

Çarşema Sor pîroz be. — Happy Red Wednesday.

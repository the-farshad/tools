16 December 1946

Fall of the Republic of Kurdistan

Iranian forces re-occupy Mahabad after 11 months

The end of eleven months

On 16 December 1946, after the withdrawal of Soviet forces from northwestern Iran, Iranian troops entered Mahabad and re-established central authority over the area, ending the eleven-month-old Republic of Kurdistan. Qazi Muhammad, the republic's only president, did not flee; he remained in Mahabad. Three months later he was hanged in Çarçira Square.

The republic's flag — red, white, and green with a sun — survived as the basis of the Kurdistan Region's flag today.
